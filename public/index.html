Your Home Buying Checklist

Track your progress through every step of the buying process

Step 1

Get Pre-Approved for a Mortgage

Before you start looking at properties, get pre-approved for a mortgage. This tells you exactly how much you can afford and shows sellers you're a serious buyer.

What You'll Need for Pre-Approval:

  • Proof of income: Pay stubs, T4s, or Notice of Assessment
  • Employment verification: Letter from employer
  • Credit check: Lender will pull your credit score
  • Down payment proof: Bank statements showing savings
  • ID: Government-issued identification

Down Payment Requirements in Canada

Purchase Price under $500,000 5% minimum
$500,000 - $999,999 5% on first $500K, 10% on remainder
$1,000,000 and above 20% minimum
üí°

Pro Tip: If your down payment is less than 20%, you'll need CMHC mortgage insurance, which adds to your costs.

Step 2

Search for Properties

Now the fun part! Use Real Estate Direct to search for properties that match your criteria across Canada.

Consider These Factors:

  • Location: Proximity to work, schools, transit, amenities
  • Property type: Detached, semi, townhouse, condo
  • Size: Bedrooms, bathrooms, square footage
  • Age & condition: New build vs. resale, renovation needs
  • Future potential: Neighbourhood development, resale value

Ready to Start Searching?

Browse available properties in your desired area.

Step 3

Make an Offer

Found a property you love? It's time to submit a formal offer. Real Estate Direct generates all the legally required forms for your province.

Your Offer Will Include:

  • Offer price: What you're willing to pay
  • Deposit amount: Usually 3-5% of purchase price
  • Closing date: When you'll take possession
  • Irrevocable date: How long seller has to respond
  • Conditions: Financing, inspection, lawyer review, etc.
  • Chattels & fixtures: What's included in the sale
‚ö†Ô∏è

Important: An offer is a legally binding contract. Once accepted, you're committed to the purchase (subject to your conditions).

Understanding Conditions

Conditions protect you as a buyer. Common conditions include:

üí∞
Financing Condition

Usually 5 business days. Allows you to secure final mortgage approval.

üîç
Home Inspection

Usually 7 days. Professional inspection reveals hidden issues.

‚öñÔ∏è
Lawyer Review

Usually 3 business days. Your lawyer reviews the contract.

üè†
Sale of Property

If you need to sell your current home first.

Step 4

Fulfil Your Conditions

Once your offer is accepted, you enter the "conditions period." This is your chance to complete due diligence before the sale becomes firm.

Conditions Period Checklist:

‚òê Finalize mortgage with your lender
‚òê Schedule and complete home inspection
‚òê Have lawyer review all documents
‚òê Arrange home insurance (required by lender)
‚òê Sign waiver of conditions before deadline
üí°

Pro Tip: If the home inspection reveals major issues, you can negotiate repairs, a price reduction, or walk away from the deal.

Step 5

Close the Deal

Closing day is when ownership officially transfers to you. Your lawyer handles most of the heavy lifting.

Before Closing Day:

  • Provide remaining down payment to lawyer's trust account
  • Sign mortgage documents with your lender
  • Do a final walkthrough of the property
  • Set up utilities in your name
  • Arrange for moving day

Closing Costs to Budget For

Land Transfer Tax Varies by province
Legal Fees $1,000 - $2,500
Title Insurance $300 - $500
Home Inspection $400 - $700
Adjustments Property tax, utilities, etc.

Your Home Selling Checklist

Track your progress through every step of the selling process

Step 1

Prepare Your Home for Sale

First impressions matter! A well-prepared home sells faster and often for a higher price.

Pre-Listing Checklist:

  • Declutter: Remove personal items and excess furniture
  • Deep clean: Every room, including windows and carpets
  • Minor repairs: Fix leaky faucets, squeaky doors, chipped paint
  • Curb appeal: Landscaping, front door, mailbox
  • Neutralize: Fresh paint in neutral colours
  • Stage key rooms: Living room, kitchen, master bedroom
üí°

Pro Tip: Consider getting a pre-listing home inspection. It helps you identify and fix issues before buyers discover them.

Repairs with Best ROI

Fresh paint (interior) 100-150% ROI
Kitchen updates 75-100% ROI
Bathroom refresh 70-90% ROI
Flooring update 70-80% ROI
Step 2

Price Your Property Right

Pricing correctly from the start is crucial. Overpricing leads to stale listings; underpricing leaves money on the table.

How to Determine Fair Market Value:

  • Comparable sales: What have similar homes sold for recently?
  • Active listings: What's your competition priced at?
  • Market conditions: Is it a buyer's or seller's market?
  • Unique features: Upgrades, location, lot size
  • Time on market: Price for how quickly you need to sell
‚ö†Ô∏è

Warning: Homes that sit on the market too long (30+ days) often sell for less than asking price. Price it right from day one.

Pricing Strategies:

  • At market value: Fair price, steady interest, normal negotiation
  • Slightly below market: Creates urgency, potential multiple offers
  • Offer date strategy: Set a date to review offers, often drives competition
Step 3

Create Your Listing

A great listing attracts more buyers. High-quality photos and a compelling description are essential.

Essential Listing Elements:

  • Professional photos: At least 15-20 high-quality images
  • Detailed description: Highlight key features and upgrades
  • Accurate measurements: Room sizes, lot dimensions
  • Property history: Year built, renovations, new systems
  • Neighbourhood info: Schools, transit, amenities
üì∏

Photo Tips: Shoot on a bright day, use wide-angle lens, stage each room, capture curb appeal, and highlight special features like fireplaces or views.

Step 4

Review & Negotiate Offers

When offers come in, review each carefully. Price isn't everything ‚Äì terms and conditions matter too.

Evaluating an Offer:

  • Offer price: How does it compare to asking price?
  • Deposit amount: Larger deposits show serious intent
  • Conditions: Fewer conditions = less risk of deal falling through
  • Closing date: Does it work with your timeline?
  • Financing: Is the buyer pre-approved?
  • Chattels included: What are they asking you to leave?

Your Response Options

Accept Agree to all terms as presented
Counter Propose different terms (price, date, conditions)
Reject Decline the offer entirely
üí°

Multiple Offers? You can ask all buyers to submit their "best and final" offer, then choose the strongest one.

Step 5

Close the Sale

Once an offer is accepted and conditions are waived, you're on your way to closing. Here's what happens next.

Seller's Closing Checklist:

‚òê Hire a real estate lawyer (if you haven't already)
‚òê Gather all property documents (deed, survey, permits)
‚òê Pay off your existing mortgage
‚òê Cancel home insurance after closing
‚òê Notify utilities of final meter readings
‚òê Complete final cleaning and move out
‚òê Leave all keys, remotes, and documents for buyer

Seller's Closing Costs:

  • Legal fees: $800 - $1,500
  • Mortgage discharge: $200 - $400
  • Status certificate (condos): $100 - $200
  • Capital gains tax: If not principal residence
  • Moving expenses: Variable
üí∞

Getting Paid

On closing day, your lawyer will pay off your mortgage, deduct closing costs, and transfer the remaining funds to your bank account. This typically happens the same day or next business day.

Important Tax Considerations

Principal Residence Exemption

If the property was your principal residence for every year you owned it, you won't pay capital gains tax on the profit.

Capital Gains Tax

For investment properties, 50% of your profit is added to your income and taxed at your marginal rate.

Non-Resident Sellers

If you're not a Canadian resident, a portion of the sale proceeds may be withheld for taxes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about buying and selling real estate with Real Estate Direct

What is Real Estate Direct? +

Real Estate Direct is a platform that enables Canadians to buy and sell real estate directly, without traditional real estate agents. We provide all the tools, forms, and guidance needed to complete transactions legally and efficiently.

Is this service available across Canada? +

Yes! Real Estate Direct is available in all 13 provinces and territories. We provide province-specific legal forms and tax calculations for each jurisdiction.

Do I need a real estate agent? +

No, you don't need a real estate agent to use our platform. Our tools guide you through the entire process. However, we strongly recommend engaging a real estate lawyer to review all documents and handle the closing.

How do I make an offer on a property? +

Once you find a property you like, click "Make Offer" to submit your offer. You'll need to specify your offer price, deposit amount, closing date, and any conditions. The seller will be notified immediately.

What conditions can I include in my offer? +

Common conditions include:

  • Financing condition (typically 5 business days)
  • Home inspection (typically 7 days)
  • Lawyer review (typically 3 business days)
  • Sale of your current property

What is a deposit and when do I pay it? +

A deposit shows good faith and is typically 1-5% of the purchase price. You pay it after your offer is accepted, usually within 24-48 hours. It's held in trust by a lawyer until closing.

What are the closing costs I should expect? +

Buyer's closing costs typically include:

  • Land transfer tax (varies by province)
  • Legal fees ($1,500 - $3,000)
  • Title insurance ($300 - $500)
  • Home inspection ($400 - $600)
  • Appraisal (if required by lender)

Use our calculator tool for accurate estimates based on your province.

How do I list my property? +

Create an account, then click "List Your Property." You'll enter property details, upload photos, and set your asking price. Your listing goes live immediately and is visible to all buyers on the platform.

How much can I save compared to using an agent? +

Traditional real estate commissions are typically 5-6% of the sale price, split between buyer's and seller's agents. Our platform fee is only 1%, meaning you could save 4-5% on a sale. On a $500,000 home, that's a savings of $20,000-$25,000!

How do I respond to offers? +

When you receive an offer, you'll be notified by email. You can accept, reject, or counter the offer directly through your dashboard. For counter-offers, you can adjust the price or terms.

What documents do I need to sell my property? +

You'll need:

  • Property title/deed
  • Recent property tax statement
  • Survey (if available)
  • Condo documents (if applicable)
  • Disclosure statement

Our platform generates the required legal forms for your province.

What are the platform fees? +

Our platform charges a 1% commission on the final sale price, payable by the seller upon successful closing. There are no upfront fees for listing your property.

When do I pay the platform fee? +

The platform fee is invoiced after your transaction successfully closes. Payment is due within 30 days and can be made via credit card or bank transfer through our secure payment system.

Are there any hidden fees? +

No hidden fees. The 1% platform commission is the only fee we charge. You may have other standard closing costs (legal fees, title insurance, etc.) but these are not charged by us.

Do I need a lawyer? +

Yes, we strongly recommend (and in some provinces it's required) that both buyers and sellers engage a real estate lawyer. The lawyer handles title searches, mortgage registration, and the actual transfer of funds and property.

Are the documents generated by the platform legally binding? +

Our platform generates province-specific legal forms based on standard templates. However, we always recommend having a lawyer review all documents before signing. The forms become legally binding once properly signed by all parties.

How does the e-signature process work? +

Our platform integrates with secure e-signature services. When a document is ready for signing, you'll receive an email notification. You can review and sign documents electronically, which is legally valid in Canada.

What happens if there's a dispute? +

Most disputes can be resolved between the parties with the help of their lawyers. For issues related to the platform itself, please contact our support team. We encourage all parties to maintain written communication through the platform for documentation purposes.

Our support team is here to help you navigate the real estate process.
